New preprint: a creativity test that predicts scientific ideation

Samuel Schapiro·May 13, 2026

Today we released Assessing the Creativity of Large Language Models: Testing, Limits, and New Frontiers — a follow-up to our earlier work on creativity-test validity that motivated the Creative AI Index.

What we found

We ran the first large-scale, systematic study of human creativity tests applied to LLMs, across three target constructs: creative writing, divergent thinking, and scientific ideation. Two main findings:

  • The Divergent Association Task is the best predictor of creative writing ability; the Conditional DAT is the best predictor of divergent thinking. But these are construct-specific — no single existing test predicts all three constructs well.
  • Contrary to popular belief, no existing creativity test reliably predicts scientific ideation ability in LLMs.

What we propose

We introduce the Divergent Remote Association Test (DRAT) — a vocabulary-space test that measures both convergent and divergent thinking in a single instrument. The DRAT is the first creativity test that significantly predicts LLM scientific ideation ability, and its performance gain is not recoverable from any linear combination of the Divergent Association Task and the Remote Associates Test. Assessing both convergent and divergent thinking in the same instrument turns out to be essential.

Why it matters

The Creative AI Index was built to make creativity-test validity visible and comparable across the field. The DRAT is a step toward filling the most visible gap on the leaderboard: there is now a creativity test that reliably predicts the construct that had previously remained out of reach.

The paper is co-authored with Alexi Gladstone, Jonah Black, and Heng Ji (University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign). Read the preprint on arXiv.
