Samuel Schapiro

I am an interdisciplinary scholar studying the science of intelligence. My work bridges cognitive science, computer science, and the philosophy of science to advance the following research directions:

  • Developing novel AI methods and systems that exhibit creative behavior.
  • Uncovering the fundamental, scientific principles of creativity governing minds and machines.

Previously, I was a visiting researcher at the Simons Institute for the Theory of Computing, Berkeley. I did my undergrad at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, where I studied theoretical computer science.

Research Areas

I. Measuring the Creativity of AI

Benchmarks and tests of creativity, especially more complex forms such as transformational and scientific creativity.

  • TwistBench: Benchmarking Transformational Creativity in LLMs via Literary Plot Twists S. Schapiro, A. Gladstone, H. Ji, R. E. Beaty Under review, COLM 2026 Workshop on Scientific Understanding of Foundation Models.
  • Assessing the Creativity of Large Language Models: Testing, Limits, and New Frontiers S. Schapiro, A. Gladstone, J. Black, H. Ji Preprint
  • Do Semantic Distance Tests Actually Predict Creativity in Large Language Models? S. Schapiro, A. Gladstone, J. Black, H. Ji Accepted at the ICML 2026 Workshop on Generative AI & Creativity. Under review, NeurIPS.

II. Improving the Creativity of AI

Methods and systems that make models more creative.

  • CreativityNeuro: Steering Language Model Weights to Improve Divergent Thinking and Reduce Mode Collapse S. Schapiro, C. Francisco Park, F. Sosa, L. R. Varshney Accepted at the ICML 2026 Workshop on Generative AI & Creativity. Under review, COLM.
  • Spark: A System for Scientifically Creative Idea Generation A. Sanyal, S. Schapiro, S. Shashidhar, R. Moon, L. R. Varshney, D. Hakkani-Tur Proceedings of the 16th International Conference on Computational Creativity (ICCC)

III. Theories and Foundations of Creativity

Formal accounts of what creativity is and how it manifests in minds and machines.

  • Artificial Creative Intelligence S. Schapiro, R. E. Beaty Invited Chapter, Cambridge Handbook of Creativity (3rd ed.), Cambridge University Press. Eds. J. C. Kaufman & R. J. Sternberg. In preparation.
  • Combinatorial Creativity: A New Frontier in Generalization Abilities S. Schapiro, S. Shashidhar, A. Gladstone, J. Black, R. Moon, D. Hakkani-Tur, L. R. Varshney Preprint
  • Transformational Creativity in Science: A Graphical Theory S. Schapiro, J. Black, L. R. Varshney Proceedings of the 16th ICCC — Best Short Paper Award
